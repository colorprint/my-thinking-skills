# Banned Words & Phrases

Hard ban in narration and long-form content. These are statistically overrepresented in AI-generated text and are exactly what AI-text detectors weight, so they earn a stronger ban than the generic vocabulary list.

## Scope

This file is the **phrase and structural blacklist:** the specific phrasings and stylistic tells that flag prose as AI. The generic AI-vocabulary list (delve, testament, vibrant, tapestry, underscore, intricate, pivotal, landscape-as-metaphor, and the rest) lives in [edit-catalog.md](edit-catalog.md) as the AI-vocabulary pattern, sourced from Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing."

**Some overlap is intentional.** A handful of words below (delve, tapestry, testament, underscores, landscape) also appear in the edit catalog's AI-vocabulary pattern. They're kept here because narrative prose needs them banned with stronger force than the generic nonfiction calibration provides.

**What's unique here:** the phrase blacklist (`"It was as if..."`, `"Little did X know..."`, `"couldn't help but"`, `"with bated breath"`, `"It's worth noting"`, `"At the end of the day"`), the structural-tells taxonomy (Bold:colon, says-everything-means-nothing, corporate-therapist voice, neat-bow endings), and character-voice calibration. None of this is in the generic edit catalog.

## Word Blacklist

- delve
- unpack / dive into / explore (as filler verbs; fine when literal)
- tapestry (as metaphor)
- testament to
- beacon of
- a symphony of / a dance of / a kaleidoscope of
- echoed through / reverberated through (as emotional metaphor)
- palpable (especially "the tension was palpable")
- navigate + abstract noun ("navigate the complexities")
- landscape (as business or tech metaphor, "the ever-changing landscape")
- synergies / leverage (as verb) / holistic / learnings
- underscores / signals that / illuminates (as analysis verbs)
- myriad / juxtaposition / dichotomy (unless in character dialogue)
- genuinely / honestly / straightforward (hedging adverbs)
- steeled himself/herself

## Phrase Blacklist

- "It was as if..."
- "Little did [character] know..." / "Unbeknownst to..."
- "In that moment, [character] realized..."
- "A myriad of"
- "couldn't help but"
- "sent shivers down [his/her] spine"
- "The weight of [abstract noun] settled on [character's] shoulders"
- "with bated breath"
- "It's worth noting that..." / "At the end of the day..."
- "Moreover" / "Furthermore" / "Additionally" (transition crutches; just start the sentence)

## Structural Tells (Not Words, But Patterns)

- **Bold-colon bullet format.** "**Clarity:** We believe in transparency." Screams AI. Use plain prose or varied formatting.
- **"Says everything, means nothing" sentences.** "This powerful approach enables teams to unlock their full potential." Delete and replace with a specific claim.
- **Corporate-therapist voice.** "I hear you. And that's valid. Let's unpack that." Write direct, not therapeutic.
- **Neat-bow endings.** Wrapping up with a feel-good restatement of the opening. Life doesn't resolve, and neither should your writing.

## Conditional Use (dialogue, ironic narration, or character voice only)

- "Needless to say"
- "To say the least"
- "Only time would tell"

## British Spellings (always flag)

colour, favour, honour, centre, grey, organised, recognised, realised, catalogued, amongst, whilst, towards (use "toward")
