# Domains — same core, different weighting

The five-part core (voice, VAKS, rhythm, specificity, story-first) applies everywhere. Each domain just leans on different parts. For a specific project, layer that project's own style guide on top of these.

## Fiction (novels, short stories)
- VAKS is the **scene-level** test: every scene relived, not reported.
- POV-colored vocabulary and dialogue logic chains matter most here.
- Use `references/prose-craft.md` heavily — it's tuned for narrative.
- "Too much" is usually unfamiliar, not wrong; don't over-sand the voice.

## Articles / essays / blog posts
- The **opening anecdote** must be relived (VAKS); the lesson arrives after the reader has felt something.
- Hook → Struggle → Breakthrough → Application — story first, principle last. Never invert into a sermon.
- Conversational voice works here (unlike fiction, where you hold the POV character's voice).
- Watch the nonfiction tells hardest: significance inflation, signposting ("let's dive in"), inline-header lists, rule-of-three.

## Marketing / testimonials / landing copy
- Apply the core + the storytelling formula. Testimonials are tiny stories — give them VAKS.
- Specific outcome over vague benefit ("cut our deploy time from 40 min to 4" beats "boosts productivity").
- Kill promotional language (the edit catalog's pattern #4) — it reads as the opposite of credible.

## Faith / values content
- Lead with life, arrive at the principle: readers feel a truth when they first feel something human. Anchor before you explain.
- Keep the register sincere, not saccharine; specificity is what makes sincerity land.
- Calibrate to the audience's standard for tone and content.
